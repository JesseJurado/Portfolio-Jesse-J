Comix

The Problem

Comic book readers struggle with fragmented platforms and inconsistent access, making it difficult to discover new series, purchase comics seamlessly, and engage with their favorite titles. This disjointed experience limits their ability to fully enjoy and explore the world of comics.

The Solution

Comix offers a wide selection of comics from various publishers, personalized recommendations, robust community features, and exclusive digital content. Our seamless platform enhances discovery, purchasing, and engagement, creating a satisfying and integrated comic book experience for users.

My Role: UX Research
UX Designer

Tools: Figma

Timeline: 1 Month

The Process

1. Research

Behavioral Archetype

Me and my team began by hypothesizing Behavioral Archetypes based on initial assumptions about comic book readers. These archetypes were refined and validated later in the process through user research and insights gathered from interviews and surveys.

Proto Persona

Me and my team created a proto-persona to establish a clear understanding of our app's target user.

Research Plan

I created a research plan for my team to gather data effectively from user interviews. Our research goals focused on understanding how users interact with comics, and our methods included interviews and surveys.

Interview Plan

My team and I created these key research questions for our user interviews. Our interview plan included open-ended questions to explore how users discover, purchase, and read comics.

Survey questions

My team and I created a survey using Google Forms to collect large-scale data, identify trends and patterns, and validate our assumptions.

Servey Results

Analyzing our survey results, we found that many users still value printed comics despite the digital age. Surprisingly, a significant number read comics monthly, highlighting a lasting appreciation for physical formats. These insights will help us cater to both traditional and modern comic enthusiasts.

Affinity Diagram

Using data from our interviews, we organized and analyzed key patterns, habits, and insights to help us create a user-centered design.

User Insight Statement

Using our findings from the affinity diagram, my team and I developed a User Insight Statement to identify what users needed most.

Empathy Map

After gaining more insight into our users, my team and I took our proto persona and created an empathy map to refine our understanding of what a potential user feels, thinks, says, and does.

POV Statement

We created a Point of View (POV) Statement to define the core problem users face. Research showed that comic book fans struggle to find and buy specific issues due to scattered platforms and poor search options. This insight helped us focus on designing a simple, user-friendly platform that makes searching, purchasing, and connecting easier.
