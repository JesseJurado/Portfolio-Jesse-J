Community Gardens

Connecting Users to Fresh Produce and Local Gardens

The Challenge

Problem: Users struggle to find affordable fresh, organic produce in grocery stores due to high prices and limited availability. This creates a barrier to maintaining a healthy and eco-friendly lifestyle.

Insight: People within a community need affordable, accessible produce because many cannot start their own gardens.

Problem Statement:
Our app, Growing Communities, was designed to help users access fresh produce, connect with their communities, and learn how to start their own gardens. However, we found that the app wasn’t fully promoting accessibility or education, leading users to continue relying on store-bought produce.

How might we: Improve the app so users are more successful in starting their gardens and connecting with their communities?

The Solution

Value Proposition:
Growing Communities bridges the gap between expensive store-bought produce and local, sustainable gardening solutions.

It provides:

  • Affordable fresh produce with flexible scheduling.
  • Connection to local communities for a more personal experience.
  • Resources to help users start their own gardens.

Key Features:

  • Easily navigable UI to find and purchase produce.
  • Messaging feature to communicate with garden owners.
  • Built-in map to locate gardens.
  • Garden-specific pages with detailed information.
  • Options for online or in-person payments.

My Role: UX Research
UX Designer

Tools: Figma

Timeline: 2 Weeks

The Process

1. Research

Proto-Persona

During the research phase, my team and I created a proto-persona to envision what a potential user of the community garden app might look like. This step helped us outline assumptions about the target audience.

Interview Plan

We also developed an Interview Plan to gather real data and gain a deeper understanding of users' needs, wants, and pain points.

User Insight Statement: "Access to affordable, fresh produce is vital, and the app must simplify gardening and community engagement."

Affinity Map

Using data from our user interviews, we identified and organized patterns to better understand our users and build a more accurate persona.

Empathy Map

After conducting interviews and organizing our data using an affinity map, we created an Empathy Map to synthesize what we learned about a specific type of user.

User Persona

We then iterated on our proto-persona, refining it into a detailed user persona, and used affinity mapping to organize our data. This allowed us to identify patterns and overlapping insights from the interviews.

2. Definition & Ideation

Problem statement/HMW

This section highlights our focus on improving Growing Communities based on user feedback. While the app was designed to help users grow their own gardens, access fresh produce, and connect with their community, we discovered areas where it wasn't meeting those goals effectively. This led us to explore ways to enhance accessibility and education to better support our users' success.

I Like..., I Wish..., What If...

As a team, we used the 'I Like..., I Wish..., What If...' method to generate constructive feedback based on our problem and 'How Might We' statements. This approach allowed us to brainstorm ideas in a structured and positive way, guiding our next steps effectively.

Moscow

My team and I used insights from our “I Like, I Wish, What If” feedback to determine which features to prioritize.

User flow

This is the user flow my team and I created to map out how our app would function. This step was essential in helping us visualize the user journey, understand how the app would work, and identify key features to include. It also allowed us to streamline our design process by ensuring the flow was intuitive and aligned with user goals.

User scenario

This user scenario was created using our persona to bring it to life, helping us understand how users would interact with our app. It allowed us to visualize the user’s journey and identify key features and functionalities that align with their needs and goals.

Storyboard

Building on the previous work my team and I completed, I created a storyboard based on our user scenario. This storyboard depicts how our user persona interacts with the app to achieve her goal of obtaining fresh produce and connecting with her community. By mapping out these interactions, we gained valuable insights into the user journey, helping us refine the app’s design to better meet user needs and expectations.

User Path

Using all the work we had completed, I created a user path to visualize the steps a user takes to complete a specific task. This helped us understand the flow of interactions from the user's perspective and identify opportunities to enhance their experience.

3. Prototyping

Paper Prototype

Once I had a clear understanding of our users and the features we wanted, I created a paper prototype to visualize the app and begin shaping the user experience.

Usability Testing Plan

After creating paper prototypes, my team and I developed a simple usability testing plan to gather insights on our designs. This provided structure and focus to our sessions, ensuring we asked the right questions to maximize efficiency.

Usability Testing Results

Using our usability testing plan, I began testing with random users. This helped us understand what users liked, disliked, and how we could iterate to better meet their needs.

4. Iteration

Mid-fidelity wireframe

Once I completed guerrilla testing, I began analyzing the necessary changes for my prototype wireframes. Using the findings from usability testing, I created a mid-fidelity wireframe to refine the design and develop a clearer vision for the Community Garden app. During this process, I also addressed pain points identified in the initial testing to improve the user experience.

Before And After

This highlights key areas for users and showcases the changes needed to improve their experience.

Final Thoughts

Outcome:
The process of designing the Growing Communities app allowed me to deeply engage with users and iterate on solutions that truly met their needs. By focusing on user feedback and testing at each stage, I was able to create a design that was not only functional but also intuitive and user-friendly. This project showcased the importance of collaboration, research, and iteration in delivering a product that aligns with user goals. From initial interviews to the final prototype, every step helped bring this vision to life, making it easier for users to access fresh produce, connect with their communities, and start their own gardens. With these improvements in place, the app is positioned to provide an impactful and seamless experience for users, and I look forward to applying the lessons learned to future projects.

What We Learned:

  • Simplicity is key: Clear onboarding and easy navigation were essential.
  • Users valued community-building features like messaging and events.
  • Trust and transparency in produce sourcing were major factors for user satisfaction.
