CommunityGardens :
Connecting Users to Fresh Produce and Local Gardens

The Challenge

Problem: Users struggle to find affordable fresh, organic produce in grocery stores due to high prices and limited availability. This creates a barrier to maintaining a healthy and eco-friendly lifestyle.

Insight: People within a community need affordable, accessible produce because many cannot start their own gardens.

Problem Statement:
Our app, Growing Communities, was designed to help users access fresh produce, connect with their communities, and learn how to start their own gardens. However, we found that the app wasn’t fully promoting accessibility or education, leading users to continue relying on store-bought produce.

How might we: Improve the app so users are more successful in starting their gardens and connecting with their communities?

The Solution

Value Proposition:
Growing Communities bridges the gap between expensive store-bought produce and local, sustainable gardening solutions.

It provides:

  • Affordable fresh produce with flexible scheduling.
  • Connection to local communities for a more personal experience.
  • Resources to help users start their own gardens.

Key Features:

  • Easily navigable UI to find and purchase produce.
  • Messaging feature to communicate with garden owners.
  • Built-in map to locate gardens.
  • Garden-specific pages with detailed information.
  • Options for online or in-person payments.

My Role: UX Research
UX Designer

Tools: Figma

Timeline: 2 Weeks

The Process

1. Research

Conducted 7 interviews with potential users. Asked questions about their gardening experience, access to fresh produce, and preferences for buying organic produce.

Key findings: Users want an intuitive app to find trustworthy produce, easy communication with sellers, and accessible gardening resources.

2. Synthesis

Created affinity diagrams, empathy maps, and iterated proto personas into user personas.

User Insight Statement: "Access to affordable, fresh produce is vital, and the app must simplify gardening and community engagement."

3. Ideation

Designed user flows, user scenarios, and storyboards to visualize the app experience. Created a MoSCoW Matrix to prioritize features like mapping gardens, order confirmations, and user messaging.

Brainstormed How Might We Statements to improve accessibility and user success.

4. Prototyping

Designed low fidelity paper prototypes showcasing the app’s core functionality. Incorporated paper prototypes and guerrilla testing for initial feedback.

5. Testing

Conducted usability testing to refine the onboarding process, navigation, and communication features. Used "I like, I wish, What if" feedback sessions to improve user satisfaction.

6. Iteration

Adjusted features based on user input:

  • Enhanced map and garden information.
  • Streamlined produce browsing and payment methods.
  • Fostered community engagement through events and tool-sharing.

The Impact

Outcome:
Final prototype demonstrated seamless usability, trustworthy features, and meaningful community connections. A/B testing confirmed improved user retention and satisfaction.

Conclusion:
Growing Communities redefines how users access fresh produce and connect with their communities. By combining education, accessibility, and sustainability, the app empowers users to embrace gardening and healthier lifestyles. It’s more than just an app; it’s a tool for building stronger, greener communities.

What We Learned:

  • Simplicity is key: Clear onboarding and easy navigation were essential.
  • Users valued community-building features like messaging and events.
  • Trust and transparency in produce sourcing were major factors for user satisfaction.
