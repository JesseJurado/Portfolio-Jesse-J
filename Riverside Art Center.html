Riverside Art Center

Challenge

Our team set out to find a nonprofit organization with a website in need of improvement—one that could benefit from a more user-centered, modern design.

The Riverside Art Center’s existing site was outdated, hard to navigate, and didn’t effectively support its mission. Users struggled to find key information, and the overall experience felt cluttered and uninviting.

Solution

We rebuilt the website with a strong focus on UX, aligning it with Riverside Art Center’s mission to promote fine arts in the Wapakoneta area. The layout is now cleaner, easier to navigate, and more engaging for users.

We also made the site fully responsive for mobile devices, helping the center reach more people and further supporting the organization’s goal.

The Process

1. Research

RAC mission statement

We began by researching the organization to better understand its mission and goals. Our goal was to design a user experience that aligned with the Riverside Art Center’s core purpose. Through our research, we found that the organization is dedicated to inspiring creativity and promoting fine arts within the greater Wapakoneta area.

Website Redesign Impact on Business Goals

The redesign of the Riverside Art Center website will enhance its ability to fulfill its core mission of promoting fine arts in the greater Wapakoneta area. The current website faces challenges in terms of user navigation, accessibility, and engagement. Which limits the effectiveness and reach to key target audiences such as donors, volunteers, and users in general.

By improving the user experience through streamlined navigation, clearer calls to action, and modernized design, the redesign can:

  • Increase user engagement: Users can navigate with much more efficiency.
  • Boost donation rates: Will make it easier for users to contribute.
  • Expand reach and accessibility: Optimizing the website for accessibility will ensure it serves a broader audience (mobile users).
  • Increase user trust: With a more streamlined navigation and modernized design, users can feel more secure about supporting the organization.

Problem Statement

People in the Wapakoneta area need a way to easily access information because the current website prevents users from fully engaging with the organization. Solving this will increase user engagement, boost donation rates, expand reach and accessibility, and build user trust.

Persona

After conducting research on the Riverside Art Center and its mission, we developed a user persona to help guide our design decisions. This persona represents one of the key user types likely to visit the site and engage with its offerings.

This persona helped us keep the user experience grounded in empathy. It influenced decisions such as simplifying navigation, prioritizing community content, and improving accessibility across devices.

User Journey Map

To better understand the user's experience, we created a journey map based on our persona, Kate Maskson—a stay-at-home mom seeking creative fulfillment and community connection. In her journey, Kate searches for local art classes and finds the Riverside Art Center website through Google. As she explores the homepage, she reads about RAC’s mission, browses through artists and class listings, and selects one to attend. Her positive experience at the class, along with meeting like-minded individuals, leads her to learn more about RAC, donate to the organization, and eventually become a member. This journey highlights the importance of clear navigation, engaging content, and a streamlined donation process in fostering user trust and long-term involvement.

Mind Map

Our team held a structured brainstorming session to generate ideas for the nonprofit’s website redesign. Each team member voted on the concepts they felt best aligned with the organization’s goals and user needs by placing stickers on their top picks. We then combined the highest-rated ideas to create a final mind map that guided our design direction.

Content Hierarchy

The content hierarchy on the homepage makes it challenging to quickly identify the most important information. Key elements, like the hero and body sections, are poorly organized, making it difficult for users to distinguish between different areas of the site.

  • Grouping: Related content is not consistently grouped, which leads to user confusion and disrupts the flow of navigation.
  • Call-to-Actions (CTAs): The CTAs feel underwhelming and lack prominence, reducing their effectiveness in driving user engagement.
Recommended Improvements:
  • Clearly differentiate sections to create a more structured layout.
  • Group related content, such as all calls to action, into well-defined sections to streamline the user experience and improve clarity.

Content Hierarchy Evaluation

We analyzed the site’s content hierarchy through color usage, scale, and grouping. While the bright colors and varied text sizes draw attention, inconsistent use and lack of structure make it hard for users to navigate. Grouping was also inconsistent, leading to confusion about related content. To improve clarity, we recommended refining the color palette, increasing and standardizing font sizes, and organizing similar content into clearly defined sections.

Content Inventory and Audit

To better understand the existing structure of the Riverside Art Center website, our team conducted a content inventory and audit. We reviewed each page to identify which elements were useful, outdated, redundant, or missing. This process helped us evaluate the clarity, relevance, and consistency of the site's content. From this audit, we pinpointed areas that needed improved organization, clearer messaging, and better alignment with user needs and business goals.

Brand Visual Identity

Our team crafted a refreshed visual identity to better reflect the organization’s mission and values. We introduced more legible typography to improve readability across devices, selected a modern yet conservative color palette, and defined a brand personality that feels bold, mature, and intellectual. These updates help create a more cohesive and professional presence while staying true to the organization’s character.

Brand Voice Guide

To ensure consistency across all communications, we created a Brand Voice Guide that outlines how Riverside Art Center should sound and present itself. We identified four key traits—Cooperative, Imaginative, Innovative, and Humble—and clarified how each one should be reflected in tone and messaging.

  • Cooperative: The voice should foster collaboration, build community, and encourage participation. It should never come across as disorganized or dismissive of individual contributions.
  • Imaginative: Creativity is central to the brand, especially in providing unique teaching and art experiences. The tone should feel curious and expressive—without sacrificing purpose or inclusivity.
  • Innovative: Messaging should highlight the center’s leadership and fresh ideas in the local arts space, offering value through resources that are distinctive and difficult to replicate.
  • Humble: Communication should be approachable and community-focused, prioritizing shared success over self-promotion, while still showing confidence and value.

This guide became an essential tool for crafting content—from headlines to CTAs—and helped shape a voice that feels both artistic and grounded in community.

Brand Prism

To clearly define Riverside Art Center’s brand, we developed a Brand Identity Prism. This framework helped us break down the brand’s personality and how it should be perceived by users.

  • Physique: Artistic visuals, vibrant colors, and a welcoming presence.
  • Personality: Imaginative, humble, supportive, and community-driven—building trust through authentic engagement.
  • Culture: A nurturing environment where creativity thrives, encouraging inspiration and self-expression.
  • Relationship: Fosters meaningful connections with users through trust, support, and shared artistic values.
  • Reflection: Speaks to individuals who are passionate, creative, and eager to grow and collaborate with others.
  • Self-Image: The user sees themselves as culturally enriched, inspired, and part of a collaborative, art-focused community.

This prism became a foundational guide for visual and tonal decisions throughout the redesign.

Current Site Architecture & Navigation Evaluation

Homepage and primary navigation include links to Home, About, Our Artists, Classes/Events/Exhibits, and Support Us/Membership. No dropdowns for organization.

    Issues
  • Cluttered links with no hierarchy.
  • Important information buried in the footer.
    Strengths
  • Accessible top menu provides easy navigation.
    Weaknesses
  • Navigation lacks hierarchy, making it hard to identify key actions.
    Suggested Improvements
  • Add dropdown menus for better structure.
  • Make primary navigation sticky (visible on scroll).
  • Highlight primary actions, like Support Us/Membership, for emphasis.

New Information Architecture and Navigation System

    Primary Navigation: Includes main sections (Home, About, Events) for easy access to core site content.
    Secondary Navigation: Uses a drop-down menu for specific sections (Events and Support Us) to keep the main menu streamlined.
    Footer Navigation: Links to social media, Gmail, and contact for quick access to external platforms.
    Tertiary Navigation: Adds two Call to actions on the homepage for membership and donating.

Navigation Wireframe

To support user flow and clarity across the site, we developed a clear and intuitive navigation wireframe. The structure includes primary, secondary, and tertiary navigation layers, each designed to guide users based on their goals. Dropdown menus under key sections like "Classes" and "Events" allow users to quickly explore related content, while the tertiary navigation places high-priority calls to action—“Become A Member” and “Donate”—in prominent buttons for maximum visibility. A clean footer provides quick access to essential links, reinforcing trust and usability across the site.

Sketched wireframes

To begin visualizing the structure of the site, our team developed sketched wireframes that explored early layout ideas and navigation flow. We focused on enhancing usability by introducing a simplified navigation bar and emphasizing important calls to action with high-contrast buttons. Subtle background blocks were used to separate content sections and improve visual orientation. Elements like consistent card layouts and repeated formatting for artist profiles were also included to support a cohesive and intuitive user experience.

Mid-fidelity pattern library

We created a mid-fidelity pattern library to transform our early ideas into functional, reusable design assets. This library served as a foundation for consistent visual and interactive elements across the site. Several components were also tested during our A/B sessions, allowing us to refine the design based on user feedback and ensure each pattern effectively supported the overall user experience.

Mid-Fidelity Prototypes

In this stage, we focused on refining both the navigation and layout to improve clarity and flow. The updated navigation structure makes it easier for users to explore the site, while the alternating layout of the artist profiles introduces balance and visual interest. By aligning the second artist image to the right, we created a more dynamic and consistent structure that strengthens the overall user experience across the site.

Mid-fidelity prototype with grid

To maintain structure and visual harmony, we designed our mid-fidelity wireframes using a 12-column grid system. This grid ensured consistent alignment, spacing, and balance across each page. By strategically adjusting the placement of key elements, we improved both the clarity and overall visual flow of the layout—making the design more intuitive and aesthetically pleasing.

Mobile Adaptation of Desktop Wireframes

To optimize for smaller screens, we reorganized and grouped content into more manageable, digestible sections. Our mobile wireframes utilize a 4-column grid system, maintaining consistency and alignment throughout. This approach ensures a balanced layout that keeps both visuals and text structured—delivering a seamless and visually appealing experience on mobile devices.

A/B testing plan for mobile prototype

If we increase the spacing between the redesigned cards and add margin to the columns, then the layout will appear cleaner and easier to navigate, improving user engagement. Additionally, redesigning the navigation will make it more intuitive, reducing the time it takes for users to find key information.

  • Proper spacing enhances readability, especially on smaller mobile screens, ensuring users can easily distinguish between different sections.
  • Redesigned cards to improve visual hierarchy, making key information more prominent and easier to read.
  • The added margin enhances the overall readability and aesthetic appeal, especially on larger screens where empty space contributes to clarity.
  • Redesigned navigation to make it more intuitive and accessible, improving user flow and making key sections easier to find.

Mobile prototype Variations

To evaluate how different design approaches would impact user experience, we created two versions of our mobile prototype.

  • Version A: featured our initial layout, with standard spacing, navigation, and card structure.
  • Version B: introduced more generous spacing, a simplified navigation system, and improved card hierarchy to highlight key content.

These variations allowed us to explore how layout and structure might influence readability and ease of use. The next step was to gather user feedback to determine which direction to move forward with.

A/B test findings

Our initial hypothesis was challenged by real user feedback—participants favored Design A over Design B.

  • Users found Design A more intuitive and easier to navigate.
  • Feedback pointed to minor adjustments needed on the Membership page for improved clarity.
  • Several users suggested moving social media links to the bottom of the page to reduce visual clutter.
  • One key insight was the recommendation to align artist images consistently on the Our Artists page, which we later implemented for better balance.

Style Guide

Our style guide focused on creating a clean, high-contrast design that keeps the spotlight on the art and artists.

  • Colors: We used a neutral base of black and white, complemented by soft blue, pale yellow, and light gray gradients. These subtle accents add warmth without overwhelming the content.
  • Typography: We paired ‘Sequel Sans’ and ‘Inter’ for readability, with ‘Neue Haas Grotesk’ bold for strong, clear headings.
  • Imagery: A mix of artwork and artist photos brings personality and authenticity to the site. While some images are stock-based, they serve as visual guidance for future high-quality sourcing.

This guide helped us shape a site that’s inviting, accessible, and aligned with the nonprofit’s mission.

High-fidelity Prototype

Our high-fidelity prototype incorporates revisions based on user insights from our A/B testing. We aimed to create a user-centered and business-centered design that is easy to use, understand, and effective for the nonprofit, all while supporting their mission of promoting fine arts through classes, lectures, and demonstrations.

Testing and results / moving forward

We tested our high-fidelity wireframe with users, asking questions about readability, language, and visual appeal. Overall, participants responded positively, with one main suggestion: add more color and slightly increase text width for better readability.

User Testing Findings
  • Overall Impression: Described as “calming” and “museum-like,” aligning with the nonprofit’s mission.
  • Text & Readability: Easy to read; suggestion to increase text width.
  • Visual & Brand Alignment: Visuals fit the brand; navigation is clear and intuitive.
  • Trustworthiness: Users felt the site was credible and trustworthy.
  • Improvement Suggestion: Add more color to enhance vibrancy.

Final thoughts

It challenged me to step up as a leader—guiding my team, staying organized, and making sure we stayed aligned throughout the process. This project helped sharpen my leadership, strengthened my ability to build clear information architecture, and deepened my problem-solving skills.

It also boosted my confidence as a designer. I now find myself analyzing websites with a much more critical and insightful eye, spotting usability issues and understanding how to improve them. This project didn’t just test my skills—it elevated them. I’m proud of how far I’ve come and excited to apply everything I’ve learned to future challenges.
