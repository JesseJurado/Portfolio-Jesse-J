Riverside Art Center

Challenge

Our team set out to find a nonprofit organization with a website in need of improvement—one that could benefit from a more user-centered, modern design.

The Riverside Art Center’s existing site was outdated, hard to navigate, and didn’t effectively support its mission. Users struggled to find key information, and the overall experience felt cluttered and uninviting.

Solution

We rebuilt the website with a strong focus on UX, aligning it with Riverside Art Center’s mission to promote fine arts in the Wapakoneta area. The layout is now cleaner, easier to navigate, and more engaging for users.

We also made the site fully responsive for mobile devices, helping the center reach more people and further supporting the organization’s goal.

The Process

1. Research

RAC mission statement

We began by researching the organization to better understand its mission and goals. Our goal was to design a user experience that aligned with the Riverside Art Center’s core purpose. Through our research, we found that the organization is dedicated to inspiring creativity and promoting fine arts within the greater Wapakoneta area.

Website Redesign Impact on Business Goals

The redesign of the Riverside Art Center website will enhance its ability to fulfill its core mission of promoting fine arts in the greater Wapakoneta area. The current website faces challenges in terms of user navigation, accessibility, and engagement. Which limits the effectiveness and reach to key target audiences such as donors, volunteers, and users in general.

By improving the user experience through streamlined navigation, clearer calls to action, and modernized design, the redesign can:

  • Increase user engagement: Users can navigate with much more efficiency.
  • Boost donation rates: Will make it easier for users to contribute.
  • Expand reach and accessibility: Optimizing the website for accessibility will ensure it serves a broader audience (mobile users).
  • Increase user trust: With a more streamlined navigation and modernized design, users can feel more secure about supporting the organization.

Problem Statement

People in the Wapakoneta area need a way to easily access information because the current website prevents users from fully engaging with the organization. Solving this will increase user engagement, boost donation rates, expand reach and accessibility, and build user trust.

Persona

After conducting research on the Riverside Art Center and its mission, we developed a user persona to help guide our design decisions. This persona represents one of the key user types likely to visit the site and engage with its offerings.

This persona helped us keep the user experience grounded in empathy. It influenced decisions such as simplifying navigation, prioritizing community content, and improving accessibility across devices.

User Journey Map

To better understand the user's experience, we created a journey map based on our persona, Kate Maskson—a stay-at-home mom seeking creative fulfillment and community connection. In her journey, Kate searches for local art classes and finds the Riverside Art Center website through Google. As she explores the homepage, she reads about RAC’s mission, browses through artists and class listings, and selects one to attend. Her positive experience at the class, along with meeting like-minded individuals, leads her to learn more about RAC, donate to the organization, and eventually become a member. This journey highlights the importance of clear navigation, engaging content, and a streamlined donation process in fostering user trust and long-term involvement.

Mind Map

Our team held a structured brainstorming session to generate ideas for the nonprofit’s website redesign. Each team member voted on the concepts they felt best aligned with the organization’s goals and user needs by placing stickers on their top picks. We then combined the highest-rated ideas to create a final mind map that guided our design direction.

Content Hierarchy

The content hierarchy on the homepage makes it challenging to quickly identify the most important information. Key elements, like the hero and body sections, are poorly organized, making it difficult for users to distinguish between different areas of the site.

  • Grouping: Related content is not consistently grouped, which leads to user confusion and disrupts the flow of navigation.
  • Call-to-Actions (CTAs): The CTAs feel underwhelming and lack prominence, reducing their effectiveness in driving user engagement.
Recommended Improvements:
  • Clearly differentiate sections to create a more structured layout.
  • Group related content, such as all calls to action, into well-defined sections to streamline the user experience and improve clarity.

Content Hierarchy Evaluation

We analyzed the site’s content hierarchy through color usage, scale, and grouping. While the bright colors and varied text sizes draw attention, inconsistent use and lack of structure make it hard for users to navigate. Grouping was also inconsistent, leading to confusion about related content. To improve clarity, we recommended refining the color palette, increasing and standardizing font sizes, and organizing similar content into clearly defined sections.
