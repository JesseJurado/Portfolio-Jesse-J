About Me.

What Empowers Me as a Designer

My passion for UX/UI design really clicked during a moment with my abuela. Her old Samsung phone had broken, and she got a new iPhone to replace it. While she had used iPhones before, things had changed—Apple had released updates that altered the interface and user experience in subtle but frustrating ways.

One of the biggest challenges she faced was something as simple as accessing her flashlight. The new gesture controls made it easy to accidentally swipe into the wrong menu, which confused her. As I watched her struggle with exaggerated swipes—natural for her age—I realized how small design changes can become huge barriers for people.

Seeing her frustration made me frustrated too. It made me ask: Why did this update make things harder? Why wasn’t this tested with users like her in mind?

That moment empowered me to pursue UX/UI design not just as a skill, but as a mission. I want to create experiences that are intuitive and accessible—so that no one, no matter their age or tech ability, feels left out or overwhelmed.
